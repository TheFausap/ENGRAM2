# **A/B/O Lore**

## **Biology**

In this universe, humanity evolved into three primary secondary sex designations:

**Alphas** — Biologically adapted to provide for and serve omegas during critical reproductive periods. Typically larger and stronger, experience periodic ruts. Undergo significant physiological changes in preparation for omega heats.

**Betas** — Possess only a faint, neutral scent that lacks the pheromonal potency of Alphas or Omegas. Cannot project scent intentionally or be easily tracked by it. Closer to baseline humans in many ways. Can mate with Alphas and Omegas.

**Omegas** — The biological centerpiece of reproduction and bonding. Experience cyclical heats with intense biological and psychological symptoms. Hold ultimate authority in bond formation and mate selection.

### **Reproductive Compatibility**

Pregnancy follows standard biology: **functional uterus \+ viable sperm \= pregnancy possible.**

The only exception: **Female alphas can only conceive after undergoing estrogen HRT** to activate their dormant reproductive system. Without HRT, their uterus remains inactive.

All other pairings follow baseline reproductive rules. Male betas cannot become pregnant (no uterus). Male omegas can both impregnate others and carry pregnancies themselves.

Secondary sex presentation usually manifests around puberty but can be delayed.

### **Knotting Physiology**

Knotting is a biological feature exclusive to alphas (both male and female) that serves reproductive and bonding purposes.

**Anatomy:**

* A bulbous swelling at the base of the penis that engorges during sexual activity

* Highly sensitive with concentrated nerve endings

* Locks the alpha and their partner together during ejaculation

**Duration:**

* Standard: 5-15 minutes

* During omega heat: 15-30 minutes (typically around 20 minutes) \- extended duration allows maximum nutrient absorption

* During alpha rut: 5-10 minutes \- shorter and more intense, driven to mate multiple times rather than sustain a single long knot

**Purpose:**

* Ensures the highest chance of conception by keeping semen in place

* Facilitates nutrient absorption for omegas through prolonged contact with mucous membranes

* Strengthens pair bonding through extended physical intimacy

* Triggers release of bonding hormones in both partners

**Cultural Notes:**

* Knotting toys exist specifically for knot stimulation and are widely available

* The sensitivity of the knot makes it a focal point in alpha sexual pleasure

## **Omega Heats**

Heats are a biological imperative for omegas, designed to ensure reproduction and bonding.

### **Physiological Effects:**

* Elevated Body Temperature (fevers around 101-105°F)

* Increased Caloric and Nutrient Demand

* Heightened Sensory Sensitivity

* **Psychological Shifts**: Intense nesting behaviors, hypervigilant territorial instincts, aggressive mate guarding, emotional volatility

* **Nutrient Absorption**: During knotting, omegas absorb nutrients, enzymes, and pheromonal compounds from alpha semen through their mucous membranes to help sustain them through the cycle

### **Nesting Behaviors:**

Nesting is an instinctive behavior that intensifies dramatically during heat. Omegas claim and prepare a safe space for their heat cycle.

**Location Selection:**

* Omegas choose easily defensible spaces based on their sense of safety

* If feeling unsafe: corners of rooms, closets, or enclosed spaces with limited entry points

* If feeling safe with a mate: typically the bed or another comfortable central location

**Nest Construction:**

* Circular arrangement of pillows, blankets, and soft materials

* Items are selected based on scent—both the omega's own scent and their mate's scent

* **Accepting clothing from an alpha (especially worn items) to add to a nest is a significant gesture of trust and acceptance**

* The omega will scent-mark the entire space using wrist glands and simply by spending time there, as heat scent is exceptionally potent

**Scent Saturation:**

* During heat, an omega's scent becomes thick and pervasive, naturally saturating their nesting space

* This serves as both a territorial marker and a signal to their chosen mate

* Non-bonded omegas may nest alone or with trusted friends/family for emotional support

### **Duration:**

* **Heat Frequency**: Every 6 months (twice per year) naturally, typically aligning with seasonal changes (spring and autumn most common)

* **Standard Duration**: 3-5 days

* **With Alpha Assistance**: 2-4 days

* **Suppressed**: 1-2 days

### **Post-Heat Shedding:**

**All omegas experience post-heat uterine shedding, regardless of external anatomy:**

* **Female omegas**: Post-heat bleeding occurs vaginally, similar to menstruation but tied to the heat cycle rather than monthly

* **Non-intersex male omegas**: Post-heat bleeding occurs rectally through the same passage connected to the uterus

* **Intersex male omegas**: Post-heat bleeding occurs vaginally through their functional vaginal canal

**Characteristics:**

* Occurs 1-3 days after heat ends if pregnancy did not occur

* Lighter than traditional monthly periods in baseline humans

* Duration: 2-5 days typically

* Considered a normal part of heat recovery

* Medical term: "post-cycle shedding" or "heat recovery bleeding"

**Omegas do NOT experience monthly menstrual cycles**—their entire reproductive system operates on a bi-annual heat-based schedule.

### **Suppression and Control:**

* **Suppressants**: Medications that reduce frequency, intensity, or delay heats. Skipping heats without suppressants is biologically risky and can cause hormonal crashes or prolonged heats.

* **Birth Control**: Can lighten heat symptoms but not fully prevent them without additional suppression measures. May also reduce post-heat shedding.

## **Alpha Ruts**

Ruts are periodic cycles of heightened physical and psychological needs experienced by all alphas, bonded or unbonded.

### **Characteristics:**

* **Rut Frequency**: 1-2 times per year naturally

* **Rut Duration**: 2-3 days

* **Age Effect**: Older Alphas (40s and beyond) are increasingly likely to experience only one rut per year unless heavily triggered (for example, exposure to an Omega in strong heat)

### **Physical Symptoms:**

* Elevated body temperature (fevers around 100-103°F)

* Fever and muscle tension

* Restless energy

* Increased aggression and territorial behavior

* Surges in sexual drive

* Heightened instinct to seek out compatible mates (if unbonded) or protect existing mate (if bonded)

When an alpha is bonded to an omega, their body undergoes dramatic physiological changes in preparation for their mate's approaching heat. This is a **separate biological process** from ruts and only occurs in bonded pairs.

### **Preparation Timeline:**

Begins approximately **4-6 weeks before** the omega's heat cycle, triggered by subtle pheromonal shifts only a bonded alpha can detect

### **Physical Changes:**

* **Significant Weight Gain**: 10-40+ pounds as the body stores energy and nutrients to sustain their omega through heat

* **Lactation**: Both male and female alphas produce a specialized, nutrient-rich substance colloquially called "alpha milk" designed specifically to nourish omegas during heat

* Increased appetite and carb cravings

* Heightened protective instincts specific to their bonded omega

* Enhanced scent production to comfort and calm their mate

### **Cultural Significance:**

Bonded alphas take immense pride in their ability to provide for their mates:

* Pre-heat weight gain is celebrated and shown off as proof of dedication

* "Look how much I gained for my omega" is a common brag among bonded alphas

* Alphas who fail to gain adequate weight may feel ashamed or inadequate

* The ability to lactate and nourish one's omega is seen as the ultimate expression of alpha devotion

### **Bond Formation:**

**Alpha Bite (Temporary)**:

* When an alpha bites an omega (typically during heat/rut), a shallow, temporary bond forms

* Only when an omega bites the alpha in return does a true, stable bond form

* This is the **consent mechanism** built into omega biology

* Omega bite triggers full hormonal integration, permanent scent marking, and deep emotional/telepathic connection

* **Stability**: Permanent unless actively broken through medical intervention

### **Bond Effects (Completed Bonds Only):**

* Enable Emotional Transference

* Allow Limited Telepathic Connection

* Deepen Instinctual and Emotional Attachments

* Permanent scent marking (bonded pairs smell "right" together)

* Physiological attunement (alphas can sense when their omega's heat approaches)

### **Omega Mate Selection: Feral and Absolute**

Omegas in heat become **intensely territorial and aggressive** about mate selection:

**Protective Behaviors**:

* Hissing, snarling, baring fangs at unwanted alphas

* Extreme nest guarding (will attack anyone who approaches without permission)

* Hypervigilant threat assessment

* **Violent rejection**: Omegas will attack, bite, claw, and seriously injure alphas they do not accept

* Think wild animal protecting their den—instinct overrides social conditioning entirely

### **Bond Breaking:**

Bonds—particularly abusive or non-consensual ones—can be broken with time, distance, and intervention, but the process is arduous.

**Methods**:

* Therapeutic counseling focused on emotional disentanglement

* Specialized medications that dampen bond hormones

* Court-ordered no-contact orders during bond degradation periods

* Physical relocation and protection services for survivors

**Challenges**:

* Breaking bonds is emotionally and physically painful for both parties

* Full recovery can take months to years

* Emotional aftereffects (phantom bond pain, depression, grief) are common

* **Timeline**: Minimum 6 months for shallow bonds, 2-5 years for deep bonds

* **Success Rate**: \~70% with medical support, \~40% without

## **Scruffing**

Scruffing is a biological mechanism unique to alphas and omegas, involving a specialized gland cluster located at the base of the skull where the neck meets the spine (approximately at the C1-C2 vertebrae, where major nerve clusters exist in baseline humans).

### **Anatomy:**

* **Location**: Back of the neck at the cervical spine, directly over major nerve pathways

* **Function**: When gripped or bitten, the gland releases a flood of calming hormones directly into the central nervous system

* **Effect**: Causes temporary limpness and docility, similar to scruffing a kitten or puppy

### **Proper Use:**

Scruffing works **only when the alpha or omega is calm or in a receptive state**.

**Effective Scenarios:**

* Calming a distressed but not panicked omega during heat (e.g., heat-sick with fever, unable to think clearly but not in active fight-or-flight)

* De-escalating an agitated alpha who is tense but not actively aggressive

* Soothing a bonded mate during emotionally overwhelming moments

* Used consensually during intimacy to induce relaxation

**Common Example:** An omega in heat is feverish and begging their alpha to complete the bonding bite, but the alpha knows they cannot consent in this state. The alpha scruffs them gently, flooding their system with calming hormones, allowing them to rest and recover without making irreversible decisions.

### **CRITICAL FAILURE STATE:**

**If an alpha or omega is already in active fight-or-flight mode—feeling threatened, cornered, or in survival mode—attempting to scruff them will backfire catastrophically.**

**What Happens:**

* Instead of calming, the touch triggers an **extreme feral response**

* The person will lash out violently, attack, bite, claw, and fight with maximum aggression

* Evolutionary response: prevents a predator from subduing them via scruffing

* The person cannot control this reaction—it is pure instinct

**Example Scenario:** An alpha is in a hyper-aggressive rut state and feels threatened. Their omega tries to scruff them to calm them down, but because the alpha is already in fight mode, the scruffing attempt makes them go completely feral. The alpha will attack—not out of malice, but because their hindbrain perceives it as a threat trying to immobilize them.

### **Cultural and Ethical Considerations:**

* **Consent is crucial**: Scruffing without permission outside of emergency situations is considered a violation

* Experienced partners learn to read body language to determine if scruffing will help or escalate

* Scruffing someone in fight-or-flight is considered dangerous and ignorant

* Many people enjoy consensual scruffing during intimacy as it creates a sense of safety and surrender

* Scruffing is **not effective on betas**, as they lack the specialized gland

## **Female Alpha Anatomy**

### **External Anatomy:**

* **Penis**: Naturally smaller than cis-male alphas (but still capable of knotting and ejaculation). Larger on average than a beta male penis.

* **Testes**: Fully external, in a scrotal sac. Produce sperm normally.

* **Vaginal Opening**: Exists between the urethral opening and anus, lined with nerve endings for sexual pleasure (especially G-spot stimulation). No urethra here—urethral opening is routed through the penis like in standard cis-male anatomy.

### **Internal Anatomy:**

* **Dormant Ovaries**: Female alphas are born with internal ovaries that remain inactive under normal hormonal conditions

* **Uterus**: A vestigial but functional uterus is present—small and inactive unless hormonally triggered

* **No Menstrual Cycle**: Without intervention, female alphas do not experience periods

### **Hormonal Profile:**

* Naturally high in androgens (testosterone-like hormones)

* Low estrogen baseline compared to omegas and betas

### **Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) Impact:**

If a female alpha chooses to undergo **Estrogen Hormone Therapy**:

* **Ovaries Activate**: Begin releasing eggs on a regular cycle

* **Menstrual Cycles Begin**: Light to moderate bleeding as the uterus shifts into an active reproductive role

* **Heat Cycles Replace Rut Cycles**: Instead of experiencing ruts (aggression, territorial urges, pre-heat weight gain), they begin experiencing heats (nesting, high fertility drives) similar to omegas

* **Fertility**: They become capable of carrying a pregnancy naturally

* **Genital Changes**:

  * Testosterone levels drop significantly, often resulting in reduced erectile function, genital atrophy, and inability to impregnate others—similar to transfeminine HRT effects in baseline humans

  * Vaginal tissues may become more active in self-lubrication

  * Breast development and lactation capacity may increase

## **Male Omega Anatomy and Physiology**

### **Anatomical Variations:**

Male omegas present in two distinct anatomical variations. Historically, only one variation was recognized, leading to centuries of medical erasure and discrimination that persists in some communities today.

### **External Anatomy:**

**Non-Intersex Male Omegas (most common, \~85% of male omegas)**:

* **Penis**: Typical in size and function, usually smaller on average than male alphas and betas

* **Testes**: External, functional, sperm-producing. Can impregnate individuals with functional uteruses.

* **No vaginal opening**

* **Slick Production**: Slick is secreted internally through specialized glands located inside the rectum. During heat, the anal canal becomes more elastic, lubricated, and sensitive, allowing for easier penetrative sex without damage.

**Intersex Male Omegas (rarer, \~15% of male omegas)**:

* Penis and external testes as normal

* **Vaginal Opening** present between the penis and anus (perineal location), fully functional for penetrative sex and natural childbirth

### **Internal Anatomy (Both Variations):**

* **Ovaries**: Present near the kidneys or lower abdomen, depending on phenotype variation. Fully functional and ovulate during heats.

* **Uterus**: Present internally and connected via a flexible, muscular channel

  * In non-intersex male omegas: connects to the rectal passage

  * In intersex male omegas: connects to the vaginal canal

### **Reproductive Pathway:**

* Fertilization occurs internally in both variations

* Embryo implants in the uterus

* Pregnancy develops normally

* **Delivery**:

  * Non-intersex male omegas: Must deliver via cesarean section

  * Intersex male omegas: Can deliver vaginally or via c-section depending on medical assessment

### **How Slick Works:**

* **Origin**: Produced by modified rectal glands (non-intersex) or vaginal glands (intersex) linked hormonally to the ovarian system

* **Purpose**: Lubricates and protects during penetrative sex, carries bonding pheromones, signals fertility to potential mates

* **During Heat**:

  * Slick becomes more abundant

  * Scent becomes more potent (sweet, musky, unique per individual)

  * The passage (rectal or vaginal) relaxes and becomes more elastic to facilitate mating without injury

### **Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) Effects:**

**Testosterone Therapy**:

* Reduces heat frequency and intensity

* May inhibit ovulation and diminish slick production

* Long-term HRT can lead to full infertility without careful medical management

* Physical masculinization occurs over time (voice deepening, muscle gain, genital growth)

**Microdosing Options**:

* Some male omegas "tune" hormone levels to preserve partial fertility while gaining some masculinizing effects

## **Cultural Bias and Discrimination Against Non-Intersex Male Omegas**

### **Historical Erasure:**

For most of recorded history, **"male omega" meant intersex presentation exclusively**. Non-intersex male omegas were:

* Completely unrecognized as omegas

* Classified as "defective betas" or "hysterical males"

* Denied access to omega-specific healthcare, legal protections, and social recognition

* Often died from pregnancy complications or untreated reproductive conditions

**Intersex male omegas** were considered the standard, "true," or "pure" form of male omega biology. All omega identity, cultural traditions, and early medical research was built around the intersex model.

### **Modern Discrimination:**

Even after the Male Omega Reproductive Rights Act (1983) legally recognized non-intersex male omegas, significant cultural bias persists:

**Traditionalist Attitudes**:

* Some families, religious groups, and conservative communities still view intersex omegas as "legitimate" and non-intersex omegas as "modern aberrations"

* Phrases like "real omega," "true omega," or "natural omega" are used as dog whistles to exclude non-intersex presentations

* Intersex omegas are prioritized in arranged matings, high-status bonds, and traditional ceremonies

**Medical Discrimination**:

* Some older physicians still show bias in treatment quality

* Fertility clinics may charge different rates or offer different services based on anatomical presentation

* The medical necessity of c-sections for non-intersex omegas is weaponized as "proof" they are "incomplete" or "defective," rather than being treated as safe, standard obstetric practice

**Social Stigma**:

* Non-intersex male omegas face accusations of being "incomplete" or "defective" for lacking a vaginal canal

* Required c-sections are used as "proof" they aren't "real" omegas (despite this being safe, standard medical practice)

* Some dating pools explicitly exclude non-intersex omegas

* Intersex omegas may enjoy privilege without recognizing it, perpetuating harm

**Cultural Tokenism**:

* Media representation of male omegas disproportionately features intersex presentations

* Traditionalist groups hold up intersex omegas as the "ideal" while erasing non-intersex experiences

* Historical figures are often assumed to be intersex in retrospectives, even without evidence

### **Internalized Bias:**

Many non-intersex male omegas struggle with:

* Feelings of inadequacy or being "lesser" than intersex omegas

* Shame around needing c-sections

* Anxiety about disclosing their anatomy to potential mates

* Imposter syndrome within omega communities

Some intersex male omegas, meanwhile, may:

* Benefit from privilege without recognizing it

* Unconsciously perpetuate bias through language or assumptions

* Feel defensive when their advantages are pointed out

### **Ongoing Activism:**

Modern omega rights groups actively fight this hierarchy:

* Education campaigns emphasizing "all omegas are real omegas"

* Legal protections against anatomical discrimination in mating contracts

* Medical training to address unconscious bias

* Media pressure for diverse omega representation

## **Historical Treatment of Male Omegas (Pre-Modern Medicine)**

### **Early Understanding (Pre-20th Century)**

**Only intersex male omegas were recognized as "male omegas"**:

* Their visible dual anatomy (penis and vaginal opening) made classification straightforward

* All omega cultural identity, laws, and medical knowledge centered exclusively on intersex presentations

* Societies built their entire understanding of "male omega" around this model

**Non-intersex male omegas were completely erased**:

* Misdiagnosed as betas suffering from hormonal "disorders"

* Heats were framed as "episodes of hysteria," "nervous conditions," or "imbalanced temperaments"

* Medical explanations ranged from demonic possession to an excess of black bile (in early humoral medicine)

**Pregnancy in non-intersex male omegas was incomprehensible to early physicians**:

* Swelling of the abdomen was misdiagnosed as gastrointestinal issues: tumors, severe constipation, parasites, or stomach ulcers

* "Treatments" often included purgatives (violent laxatives), bloodletting, or surgeries to "relieve swelling"—all of which almost universally caused miscarriage or death

**Childbirth for non-intersex male omegas was extremely lethal**:

* Before safe cesarean sections, a non-intersex male omega could not deliver vaginally

* Attempts at natural delivery failed—resulting in ruptured uteruses, sepsis, and death

* Most non-intersex male omegas who conceived died in late pregnancy or during labor, their deaths officially recorded under unrelated causes

**Intersex male omegas, meanwhile, had access to**:

* Recognition and social legitimacy

* Omega-specific healthcare (limited as it was)

* Legal protections under early omega rights laws

* Cultural acceptance and traditional mating practices

## **Turning Point: The Medical Revolution (1950s–1970s)**

### **Introduction of Ultrasound (Late 1950s–1960s):**

* Early ultrasound technology revolutionized internal imaging

* For the first time, doctors discovered male patients—presumed fully male externally—who had fully developed internal reproductive systems: ovaries, fallopian tubes, and uteri

* **Medical Neglect**: Despite ultrasound technology existing, medical institutions—dominated by Alpha and Beta physicians—showed little interest in funding research for "abnormal" presentations, mirroring historical medical neglect of cis women's health

### **Reclassification Crisis:**

* Medical journals published groundbreaking case studies confirming that secondary gender classification was far more complex than previously believed

* First published case study: **1968**

* **Identity Crisis**: The revelation that non-intersex male omegas existed challenged centuries of cultural understanding about what "male omega" meant

* Intersex omegas were suddenly no longer the only legitimate male omega presentation

* It became clear that many "betas with hormonal imbalances" were actually undiscovered male omegas who had been dying without recognition for centuries

### **Rights Movements:**

Activism surged in the **1970s**. Male omegas (both intersex and non-intersex) fought for:

* Legal recognition of heats and nesting rights for ALL anatomical presentations

* Access to safe cesarean deliveries without stigma

* Mandatory ovarian and uterine cancer screenings for all male-bodied omegas

* Anti-discrimination laws around employment, mating rights, and healthcare access

* **Explicit inclusion** of non-intersex male omegas in omega protection laws

### **Medical Guidelines Established (Late 1970s–Early 1980s):**

* Regular screening for male omega-specific conditions regardless of external anatomy

* Specialized OB/GYNs trained to work with both intersex and non-intersex male omega anatomy

* Development of targeted suppressants and fertility treatments for all male omega variations

* Standardized c-section protocols for non-intersex male omegas

### **Tragic Legacy:**

* Countless non-intersex male omegas died anonymously, misdiagnosed or mistreated

* Historical records erased many, misgendering them as "afflicted betas" or recording them simply as "unexplained deaths"

* **The cultural privileging of intersex omegas persists even into modern times**, with some regions slower to update laws, social attitudes, or medical training

* Many intersex omegas remain unaware of the historical advantages their presentation afforded them

## **The Male Omega Reproductive Rights Act (1983)**

### **Background:**

Throughout the 1960s and 70s, as ultrasound technology revealed the biological realities of non-intersex male omegas, the medical establishment initially refused to respond with systemic changes.

* Non-intersex male omegas lacked political power, social legitimacy, and legal recognition

* Early activists were dismissed as "mentally ill betas" or "unstable anomalies"

* Medical institutions prioritized research into female omega and intersex male omega reproductive health, considering non-intersex male omega pregnancies "rare anomalies" not worth widespread policy change

* Most non-intersex male omegas still died from untreated pregnancy complications, ovarian cancer, or were sterilized without consent during unrelated surgeries

### **The Parallel Crisis:**

By the late 1970s, non-intersex male omegas faced a twofold crisis:

* **Silent Epidemic**: Thousands were dying quietly from preventable reproductive health issues

* **Public Health Ignorance**: Hospitals often refused to treat pregnant non-intersex male omegas, claiming lack of protocols or fear of liability

### **The Role of Female Omegas:**

It was **female omegas**—particularly bonded ones, educated ones, politically connected ones—who forced society to listen.

* Female omega organizations (originally formed to fight for heat protection laws) expanded their missions to include ALL male omegas, regardless of anatomical presentation

* They marched, testified before lawmakers, organized hospital sit-ins, and held mass memorials for male omegas who had died without dignity

* **Shared Biology, Shared Struggle**: Female omegas used their own reproductive oppression as leverage, arguing that all omegas—regardless of external presentation—deserved protection and care

* **Emotional Impact**: Public awareness campaigns personalized non-intersex male omegas' suffering: photos, testimonies from grieving bonded partners, stories of stillborn children, and preventable cancer deaths

* **Intersex male omega allies**: Many intersex male omegas joined the movement, using their relative privilege and social legitimacy to advocate for their non-intersex counterparts

**Without female omega advocacy and intersex male omega allyship, it is likely that non-intersex male omega rights would have remained decades behind.**

### **Passage of the Act:**

In **1983**, after nearly a decade of relentless pressure, the **Male Omega Reproductive Rights Act** passed—a landmark piece of legislation that included:

* Mandatory reproductive health screening for all identified omegas, **regardless of external genitalia or anatomical presentation**

* Legal protections against medical discrimination based on secondary gender **or anatomical variation**

* Access to cesarean deliveries, fertility care, and hormonal management tailored to all male omega anatomical types

* Recognition of heats in non-intersex male omegas as a legitimate biological phenomenon

* Funding for reproductive cancer research specific to all male omega anatomies

* **Explicit prohibition** of discrimination based on intersex vs. non-intersex presentation in healthcare, employment, and legal bonding

It wasn't a perfect law—many loopholes and enforcement failures plagued its early years—but it was the first systemic acknowledgment that **all male omegas** existed and deserved care.

### **The Legacy:**

*"We mourn the lost, and we fight for the living."*  
 — Inscription on the Male Omega Memorial, Washington D.C., erected in 1991

* The Act saved thousands of lives in the decades that followed

* Intersectional alliances between male and female omegas, and between intersex and non-intersex presentations, reshaped political activism permanently

* **Stigma and bias persist** in some regions even today—but every heat clinic, every cancer screening, every safe cesarean section stands as a testament to the ones who refused to be silent

A radical activist organization composed of male omegas (both intersex and non-intersex), female omegas, and some allied betas.

* Less formal, more confrontational—pioneered "die-ins" (where activists staged mass fake deaths on government steps) to represent the uncounted dead

  * Equal medical funding for all omega anatomical presentations

  * Mandatory reproductive rights education

  * Safe birthing centers for all omegas

## **Suppressants**
